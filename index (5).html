About the Book

A story about finding yourself in the structure of ordinary days

Arjun used to be the kind of person who ironed his shirts the night before. Who made chai the right way — cardamom first, grandmother's recipe, specific order. Who ran at five-thirty in the morning and wrote in a notebook before the world woke up.

Eight months ago, all of that stopped.

Set in Mumbai and Pune, The Architecture of Days follows Arjun as he's slowly guided — by an old gardener, by the wisdom of people who've already walked through fire — back to himself. Not through grand revelations, but through the small, deliberate acts that make a life.

This is a book about routines. About the extraordinary freedom hidden inside the most ordinary disciplines. About the architecture we build — or let crumble — with every passing day.

From the Pages

Chapter One: The Morning That Broke Him

He had set the alarm three years ago during a period of his life he now thought of as Before, with a capital B... The 7:14 was specific because he'd done the math. Shower at 7:15, seven minutes. Dressed by 7:25. Chai by 7:30. Out the door by 7:48.

He used to calculate things like that. He used to iron his shirts the night before. He used to be the kind of person who thought about what version of himself would show up to the next morning.

That person had not left dramatically. He had just — thinned out, gradually, like a photograph left in sunlight, until one day Arjun had looked in the mirror and not been entirely sure who was looking back.
